🎯 Step 1 — Choose Your Niche

A profitable affiliate niche sits at the intersection of three things: what you can write about credibly, what has affiliate programmes worth promoting, and what people actively search for and buy.

💰 Step 2 — Pick Your Affiliate Offers

Aim for a mix: 1–2 high-ticket offers (£50+ commission), 2–3 mid-tier (£15–50 commission), and 1–2 recurring (monthly commission). Don't promote everything — promote what you'd genuinely recommend.

Offer Evaluation Checklist

  • Commission rate is at least 20%, or flat fee of £30+ per sale
  • Cookie window is 30 days or more (longer = more chance of attribution)
  • Product has good reviews — you've verified it works
  • The product solves a real problem for your audience
  • Affiliate dashboard shows conversion rates and EPC (earnings per click)
  • Payment is reliable — check forums, affiliate groups for red flags
  • Marketing materials available (banners, email swipes, product images)

📝 Step 3 — Map Your Content Clusters

A content cluster is a main pillar page surrounded by supporting articles that all link back to it. This structure improves SEO and guides readers towards your affiliate offer.

Cluster Structure

P

Pillar Page (main)

"Best [Product Category] in 2026 — Compared & Reviewed". 2,000+ words. Targets the broadest, most commercial keyword. Links to all supporting articles.

S

Supporting Article 1 — Review

"[Product A] Review: Is it worth it in 2026?" 1,500 words. In-depth single review. Links to pillar page and comparison articles.

S

Supporting Article 2 — Comparison

"[Product A] vs [Product B]: Which is better for [Audience]?" 1,500 words. Direct comparison. High buyer intent.

S

Supporting Article 3 — How-to

"How to [Use Product / Achieve Outcome] with [Tool]." Informational, builds trust. Links to review and pillar.

S

Supporting Article 4 — Problem/Solution

"The best way to solve [Problem]" — educational article that naturally leads to the product recommendation.

📧 Step 4 — Set Up Email Capture

Email is your most reliable distribution channel. Build a list from day one. Every affiliate site should have an opt-in connected to the content.

Email Capture Checklist

  • Choose email platform (MailerLite or Kit — both have free plans)
  • Create a lead magnet relevant to your niche (checklist, cheat sheet, template)
  • Set up landing page with opt-in form
  • Embed opt-in form in: sidebar, end of every article, exit-intent popup
  • Build 3-email welcome sequence (welcome, best resource, engagement ask)
  • Set up weekly or fortnightly newsletter cadence
  • Add affiliate links naturally inside newsletter content (not every email)

🚀 Launch Timeline — 60 Days

Days 1–14 — Foundation
  • Niche selected and validated with keyword research
  • 2–3 affiliate programmes applied to and approved
  • Domain registered and hosting set up
  • Website built (WordPress or simple static site)
  • Email platform set up with lead magnet and welcome sequence
Days 15–30 — Content
  • Pillar page written and published
  • 4 supporting articles written and published
  • All articles internally linked to pillar page
  • Affiliate links placed naturally (not forced) in relevant articles
  • Email opt-in embedded across all pages
Days 31–45 — Distribute
  • All articles shared on social (LinkedIn, X, Pinterest)
  • 2 more supporting articles published
  • Guest post opportunity or link-building started
  • Google Search Console set up and site submitted
  • First newsletter sent to subscribers
Days 46–60 — Optimise
  • Check Google Search Console for impressions and clicks
  • Identify which articles get traffic — add more affiliate CTAs there
  • A/B test different CTA placements (top, inline, end of article)
  • Review affiliate dashboard: any clicks? Any conversions?
  • Plan second content cluster based on performance data

⚖️ UK Compliance Checklist

Affiliate marketing in the UK is regulated by the ASA (Advertising Standards Authority) and the CAP Code. Non-disclosure can result in formal complaints and reputational damage.

  • All affiliate links labelled with "AD", "Sponsored", or "Affiliate link"
  • Disclosure is visible before the reader clicks — not buried in footer
  • Privacy policy mentions use of affiliate links and cookies
  • Cookie consent banner in place (UK PECR compliance)
  • No false or misleading claims about products you promote
  • If promoting financial products: FCA regulatory status disclosed
