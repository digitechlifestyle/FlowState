🤖 Part 1 — AI Trading Principles

AI in trading means using algorithms — rule-based or machine-learning — to analyse data and execute decisions faster than any human can. Understanding what AI can and cannot do is the most important foundation.

What AI Trading Actually Does

Pattern Recognition

AI scans thousands of price patterns across multiple timeframes simultaneously. It spots formations — wedges, head-and-shoulders, support/resistance — that would take a human hours to find manually.

Sentiment Analysis

Modern AI tools scan news, social media, and on-chain data to gauge market sentiment in real time. A spike in negative news about a coin can trigger a sell signal before price moves.

Speed Execution

Bots execute in milliseconds. In volatile crypto markets, a 200ms advantage over a manual trade can be the difference between a good fill and slippage. Bots don't panic, don't hesitate, don't sleep.

Backtesting

AI tools can run your strategy against years of historical data in seconds. This lets you stress-test logic before risking a penny of real money — a huge advantage manual traders don't have.

What AI Trading Cannot Do

  • Predict the future — AI finds patterns in past data, which may not repeat
  • Guarantee profits — no strategy wins 100% of the time
  • React to unprecedented events — a black swan (exchange collapse, regulatory ban) breaks models trained on normal market data
  • Remove emotional decisions entirely — you still control the strategy, risk settings, and when to shut it down
  • Replace understanding — using a bot you don't understand is like driving blindfolded
💡 The First Principle of AI Trading

If you can't explain exactly what your bot does in plain English, you shouldn't be running it with real money. Understand the logic first. Paper trade second. Real money third.

⚙️ Part 2 — Bot Types Explained

Not all trading bots work the same way. Using the wrong bot for your situation is one of the most common beginner mistakes.

Bot TypeHow It WorksBest ForRisk Level
Grid Bot Places buy and sell orders at set intervals above and below current price. Profits from sideways/ranging markets. Ranging, sideways markets. BTC/USDT in low-volatility periods. Low-Medium — loses in strong trends
DCA Bot Dollar-cost averages into a position automatically. Buys more as price drops, averages down entry cost. Long-term holders who want to accumulate. Reduces timing risk. Low — but requires conviction in the asset
Trend-following Bot Uses technical indicators (EMA, RSI, MACD) to detect trend direction. Buys in uptrends, sells in downtrends. Trending markets. Performs well during strong bull/bear runs. Medium — whipsaws in ranging markets
Arbitrage Bot Exploits price differences for the same asset across exchanges. Buys where cheaper, sells where more expensive. Experienced traders only. Requires high capital and fast execution. Medium — slim margins, high complexity
Market-making Bot Places simultaneous bid and ask orders to profit from the spread. Provides liquidity to the market. Advanced only. Requires significant capital and low-fee exchange access. High — inventory risk in volatile conditions
AI/ML Bot Uses machine learning to adapt strategy based on changing market conditions. More complex, harder to audit. Experienced users with technical knowledge to validate the model. High — model drift and overfitting risks

🔌 Part 3 — How Exchange APIs Work

An API (Application Programming Interface) is how your bot communicates with an exchange. Instead of clicking buttons manually, your bot sends instructions directly to the exchange's servers.

API Key Types — Know the Difference

Permission TypeWhat It AllowsRisk if Leaked
Read-onlyView balances, order history, market data. Cannot trade or withdraw.Low — attacker can see but not touch funds
TradePlace and cancel orders. Cannot withdraw funds.Medium — attacker can drain via losing trades
WithdrawMove funds out of the exchange.Critical — never give a bot this permission
🚨 API Security Rules — Non-Negotiable

1. Never grant Withdraw permission to any bot or third-party platform.
2. Restrict API keys by IP address — most exchanges let you whitelist your home IP.
3. Store API keys as environment variables, never hardcoded in scripts.
4. Rotate keys every 90 days or immediately after any suspected breach.
5. Use a separate exchange sub-account for bot trading — ring-fence your bot capital.

What an API Request Looks Like

You don't need to write code — but understanding the structure helps you audit what a bot is actually sending:

# Simplified example — placing a market buy order via API POST https://api.exchange.com/v1/order Headers: API-Key: your-key-here Signature: hmac-sha256-signature Body: symbol: BTC/USDT side: buy type: market quantity: 0.001 Response: order_id: 123456789 status: filled filled_price: 61450.00

Exchange API Setup Checklist

  • Create a sub-account on your exchange (separates bot funds from main holdings)
  • Generate API key with Trade permission only — no Withdraw
  • Whitelist your IP address on the API key
  • Copy key and secret into your bot platform (never screenshot them)
  • Delete the API key immediately if you stop using the bot
  • Test with a tiny amount first — £5–10 max — to confirm connection works

🛡️ Part 4 — Risk Management

Risk management is not optional. Every trader who has blown an account did so because they ignored it. The rules below aren't conservative — they're the minimum to survive long enough to improve.

The Core Rules

1
Never Risk More Than 1–2% Per Trade
If your bot capital is £1,000, max loss per trade is £10–20. This sounds tiny. It means you can lose 50 trades in a row and still have capital left. Most beginners risk 10–25% per trade and blow up within weeks.
2
Set a Maximum Drawdown Limit
Decide in advance: if my bot loses X% of its capital, it stops automatically. For beginners: 15% max drawdown. Most bot platforms let you set this. If it triggers, the bot pauses until you review what went wrong.
3
Never Use Leverage as a Beginner
Leverage (2x, 5x, 10x) multiplies both gains and losses. A 10% price move against a 10x leveraged position wipes your entire stake. Learn spot trading first. Add leverage only after 6+ months of consistently profitable spot trading.
4
Separate Your Trading Capital from Savings
Bot capital must be money you can afford to lose entirely. Keep it in a separate exchange sub-account. Never top it up from savings after a loss — that's emotional trading, and it ends badly.
5
Log Every Trade and Review Weekly
Your exchange and bot platform both provide trade history. Export it weekly. Look for patterns: which pairs are losing? Which strategy settings perform best? Improvement comes from data, not instinct.

📅 30-Day Beginner Plan

Work through this in order. The plan is deliberately slow. Rushing to real money before you understand what you're doing is how most beginners lose.

Days 1–7 — Education Only (No Money)
  • Read Part 1–4 of this guide in full — take notes
  • Set up a free account on Bitsgap or 3Commas (paper trading mode)
  • Choose ONE bot type to start with: DCA for BTC or ETH
  • Run a backtest on your chosen strategy over the last 6 months
  • Learn how to read your exchange's API documentation
  • Watch 3 YouTube videos specifically about the bot type you chose — verify claims sceptically
Days 8–14 — Paper Trade
  • Run your chosen bot in paper trade (simulated) mode for 7 full days
  • Log every simulated trade in a spreadsheet
  • Note: win rate, average profit/loss per trade, largest drawdown
  • Adjust settings if strategy loses more than 5% in paper trading
  • Register on your chosen FCA-registered UK exchange if not already done
  • Complete KYC verification — can take 1–3 days
Days 15–21 — Live With Tiny Capital
  • Deposit £25–50 into a sub-account — this is your test capital
  • Generate a Trade-only API key with IP whitelist
  • Connect bot to exchange — test with a single small order first
  • Set maximum drawdown limit to 15%
  • Let bot run for 7 days with zero interference
  • Do not adjust settings mid-week — you need a clean test period
Days 22–30 — Review and Decide
  • Export full trade history from exchange and bot platform
  • Calculate actual P&L including all fees
  • Compare live results to paper trade results — are they similar?
  • If profitable or breakeven: consider scaling to £100–200 next month
  • If losing: identify why before adding more capital — not after
  • Decide: continue this strategy, adjust it, or try a different approach
💡 The Most Important Rule in This Guide

Never add more capital to a losing bot trying to "recover" losses. If the strategy isn't working at £50, it won't work better at £500. Fix the strategy first. Scale the capital when you have consistent results — not before.
