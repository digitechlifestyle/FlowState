📝 Layer 2 — Seed Phrase Security

Your seed phrase (12 or 24 words) is the master key to your wallet. Anyone who has it, controls everything. It cannot be changed — lose it and lose access forever; expose it and lose everything.

🚨 Absolute Rules

1. Never photograph your seed phrase. Photos sync to cloud storage.
2. Never type it into any website, app, or AI chatbot.
3. Never store it in a password manager, notes app, or email.
4. Never say it out loud on a video call.
5. No legitimate wallet, exchange, or support team will ever ask for it.

How to Store It Safely

  • Write it by hand on paper — use a ballpoint pen, not pencil (fades)
  • Create two physical copies stored in different physical locations
  • Consider a fireproof, waterproof metal backup (Cryptosteel, Bilodal, or similar)
  • Store one copy in a home safe and one with a trusted family member or solicitor
  • Do not laminate paper copies — laminate can peel and obscure words over time
  • Test recovery before depositing significant funds — restore on a spare device to verify your phrase works
💡 Passphrase (25th Word)

Most hardware wallets support an optional extra passphrase — effectively a 25th word added to your seed. Even if someone finds your seed phrase, they can't access your funds without the passphrase. Use it for significant holdings. Memorise it or store separately from your seed phrase — never together.

🏦 Layer 3 — Exchange Hardening

You'll likely hold some crypto on exchanges for trading. Make your exchange account as hard to breach as possible.

1
Strong Unique Password
Use a password manager (Bitwarden is free and open-source). Generate a 20+ character random password for every exchange. Never reuse passwords. Exchange breaches are common — a reused password means one breach compromises everything.
2
Use an Authenticator App — Not SMS 2FA
SMS 2FA is vulnerable to SIM swapping (see Layer 4). Use Google Authenticator, Authy, or — best of all — a hardware security key like a YubiKey. Disable SMS 2FA wherever possible.
3
Enable Withdrawal Whitelist
Most exchanges let you whitelist specific withdrawal addresses. New addresses require email confirmation and a 24-48 hour delay. Even if someone logs in to your account, they can't instantly drain it.
4
Use a Dedicated Email Address
Create a separate email address used only for crypto exchanges — never used for newsletters, signups, or general browsing. This email has no public trail. Enable 2FA on it too.
5
Anti-Phishing Code
Coinbase, Binance, Kraken and most major exchanges let you set a personalised anti-phishing code. This code appears in every legitimate email from them. Any email without it is fake.
6
Only Use FCA-Registered Exchanges (UK)
In the UK, crypto exchanges handling UK customers must register with the FCA. Coinbase, Kraken, Bitstamp, and Gemini are all FCA-registered. Binance.com was previously delisted — use Binance UK (binance.com/en-GB) which is FCA-registered.

📱 Layer 4 — SIM Swap Protection

SIM swapping is when an attacker convinces your mobile network to transfer your number to a SIM they control. They then bypass any SMS-based 2FA on your accounts. It's more common than most people think — and targeted attacks often focus on known crypto holders.

How to Protect Yourself

  • Switch all exchange 2FA from SMS to an authenticator app or hardware key
  • Call your mobile network and add a SIM lock — a PIN required before any SIM transfer
  • EE, O2, Vodafone, and Three all offer this — ask for "SIM swap protection" or "port freeze"
  • Use a Google Voice or separate VOIP number for exchange account registration if possible
  • Do not publicise your crypto holdings on social media — you become a target
  • Check "Have I Been Pwned" (haveibeenpwned.com) to see if your email is in known data breaches
🚨 If You Think You've Been SIM Swapped

If your phone suddenly loses signal for no reason, act immediately. Call your network from another phone. Then contact all exchanges and request a temporary account lock. Time matters — most account drains happen within minutes of a SIM swap.

🎣 Layer 5 — Scam Recognition

In my experience looking at scams reported to the FCA and Action Fraud, the majority of UK crypto losses aren't from hacks — they're from social engineering. Knowing the playbook makes you almost immune.

The Most Common Attacks

Scam TypeHow It LooksRed Flag
Fake exchangeProfessional-looking site, often paid Google ad at top of resultsNot in your bookmarks. URL is slightly off (coinb4se.com)
Recovery scam"We can recover your lost crypto" — charges upfront fee, disappearsNo legitimate recovery service exists for lost seed phrases
Romance scam (pig butchering)Online relationship, gradual move to crypto "investment platform"Unsolicited contact + always profitable + pushes you to a specific platform
Fake supportResponds to your tweet/forum post, offers help via DMExchanges never reach out to you unprompted — report them
Approval phishingFake DeFi site asks wallet to approve a transactionYou didn't initiate it. Check what you're approving before signing
Airdrop scam"You've received free tokens" — click to claimUnsolicited tokens. Never interact with unknown tokens in your wallet
💡 The Universal Test

Any opportunity that promises guaranteed returns, requires urgency, or pressures you not to seek outside advice is a scam. No exceptions. Legitimate investments don't require you to act before thinking.

Safe Browsing Habits

  • Bookmark every exchange and DeFi app you use — never Google them again
  • Check the URL before entering any credentials or connecting a wallet
  • Use a separate browser profile or even device for crypto activity
  • Disable browser extensions on crypto sites — some extensions steal wallet data
  • Never click links in emails claiming to be from exchanges — go to the site direct
  • Report scam attempts to Action Fraud (actionfraud.police.uk) and the FCA

📅 4-Week Security Setup Plan

Work through this plan one week at a time. Don't rush it. By the end of week 4, you'll have the full security stack in place.

Week 1 — Foundations
  • Install Bitwarden (free) and migrate all passwords to it
  • Generate new strong unique passwords for every exchange
  • Enable 2FA on all exchange accounts using an authenticator app
  • Disable SMS 2FA on all exchanges where possible
  • Create a dedicated email address for crypto accounts only
Week 2 — Hardware Wallet Setup
  • Order a hardware wallet from the official manufacturer site only
  • Set it up on a clean device — not a shared family computer
  • Write down your seed phrase by hand, create two copies
  • Store copies in two separate physical locations
  • Test recovery before depositing any funds
  • Transfer significant holdings from exchanges to hardware wallet
Week 3 — Exchange Hardening
  • Enable withdrawal address whitelisting on all exchanges
  • Set up anti-phishing codes where available
  • Review and remove any unused exchange accounts or connected apps
  • Call your mobile network — request SIM swap protection / port freeze
  • Bookmark every exchange URL you use — delete browser history for those sites
Week 4 — Ongoing Habits
  • Check haveibeenpwned.com — update passwords for any breached emails
  • Review all wallet approvals on revoke.cash (revoke any unused DeFi permissions)
  • Set a monthly calendar reminder: review exchange permissions and app connections
  • Tell a trusted family member where your seed phrase backups are stored
  • Do NOT tell them the actual phrase — tell them the location and to contact your solicitor
